ENVIRONMENTAL ECONOMICS & DATA SCIENCE

DAN HAMMER

Dr. Dan Hammer [CV] is the Chief Technology Officer of Earthrise Alliance. He is the winner of the inaugural Pritzker Environmental Genius Award. Dan earned his PhD in environmental economics from U.C. Berkeley, where he was a Fellow at the Berkeley Institute for Data Science and won the 2019 Mark Bingham Prize for Excellence by a Young Alumnus. He previously served as a Senior Policy Advisor in the Obama White House and a Presidential Innovation Fellow at NASA. Dan was the Chief Data Scientist at the World Resources Institute, where he co-founded Global Forest Watch. He has been a National Geographic Fellow and a lead math instructor at San Quentin State Prison with the Prison University Project. Dan graduated with high honors in mathematics and economics from Swarthmore College, where he was a Lang Opportunity Scholar and a Watson Fellow.

Experience

Earthrise Alliance

Chief Technology Officer

Earthrise is an environmental nonprofit that builds product and writes policy for Earth observation data. Examples include (1) an educational application that relies on satellite imagery to address the Next Generation Science Standards; and (2) supporting journalists that report on humanitarian and environmental events with data analysis and satellite imagery. Supported stories have won top honors from the Webby Awards, the Society for News Design, and the Malofiej International Infographic Awards (the “Pulitzer of Infographics”). A project of the Earth Genome.

The White House

Senior Policy Advisor

Worked in the Office of the Chief Technology Officer within the Obama White House as Senior Policy Advisor, specializing in the public finance of data infrastructure. Spoke extensively about data infrastructure, open governance, and civic tech at conferences like the Open Government Partnership summit and subsequently outlets like Vice.

National Geographic Society

Fellow & Senior Advisor

The National Geographic Society was founded in 1888 “to increase and diffuse geographic knowledge." A critical and historical component of the organization is the National Geographic Fellows program. I was the first Fellow through the newly established National Geographic Labs, a research and innovation team to “drive new ways of exploring and understanding the world” and to “inspire actions to secure a planet in balance.”

NASA

Presidential Innovation Fellow

Served as a Presidential Innovation Fellow at NASA, reporting to the Chief Technology Officer for Information Technology, and building web infrastructure for the space agency’s stranded data assets. The work was featured in a number of articles, including The Register, the NASA Open Data Blog, and Science. Coverage of the position found in Berkeley News.
