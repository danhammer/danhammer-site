About me

Dan Hammer is a PhD Candidate at UC Berkeley, studying environmental economics and information theory. He graduated from Swarthmore College with high honors in mathematics and economics, and passed the mandatory swim test with the grace of a cuttlefish. Dan does not like self-introductions, especially those written in the third-person, so I am writing it for him. My name is Murray. And I am a human with a square jaw and strong features. [Hold it together, Murray, this isn't about you.] Fine. Dan Hammer is the co-creator of the FORMA algorithm to detect forest clearing activity, which serves as a basis data set for the Global Forest Watch initiative. He is now a Research Fellow at the World Resources Institute where he works with a team that brings coding cool to a warming planet. Dan is also an instructor at San Quentin State Prison as part of the Prison University Project. He is disgusted by the penal system in the United States, which does nothing for social justice. On that we can agree. This is Murray, signing off. For now...
